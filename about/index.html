💬 ABOUT ME

My first name is Yin Lin (颖宁 yǐngníng), my family name is Tan (陈 chén), and my pronouns are she/her. That's me on the right, in one of my favorite bookstores.

I'm a first year Linguistics PhD student at Stanford University, and I'm currently based in both Stanford, CA and Singapore. I completed my undergraduate degree at the National University of Singapore in 2020 and spent the next two years in journalism and research.

I find language fascinating, and so I guess it makes sense that I enjoy learning more about it and how it's used. My research interests primarily lie in sociolinguistics. Specifically, I focus on language variation, multilingualism and code-switching, and sociophonetics.

I speak English, Singlish, and Mandarin, used to learn French, and am learning Japanese and Korean.

In my downtime, I can be found at dance (my main genre is house), finding new music, doing yoga, consuming media, planning my next trip, or dabbling in some vaguely creative pursuit.
